About the book

Orchids of Dharamshala is a pictorial guide to the wild orchids found in Dharamshala town and the surrounding Dhauladhar hills. It is the result of over five years of exploration and documentation.

This book aims to be part of the evidence of the rich natural heritage of the Kangra valley. The fragile eco-systems that have supported orchids and other native forms of plant and insect life in these hills are increasingly at risk due to environmental changes and loss of habitat brought about by unchecked growth.

All of the 28 orchid species found in the region are richly illustrated with detailed photographs to describe their complex anatomy.

Whether you are a student of botany or simply keen to identify the orchids encountered on your hike, this guide will be a useful companion.

Meet the Author

Ashwini Bhatia writes and photographs for the Associated Press in Dharamshala, where he also runs a small cafe, Moonpeak Deli. Over the years, he has walked the surrounding hills keenly observing birds, butterflies, dragonflies, spiders and other local wildlife.

As an amateur botanist he has documented more than 500 species of flowering plants growing wild in the region. Orchids of Dharamshala is his first book.

Ashwini is currently working on a set of slim volumes on local trees of the Kangra valley. He can be reached at mcleodwild@gmail.com.
