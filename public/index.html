About the book

Orchids of Dharamshala is a pictorial guide to the wild orchids found between the lower Dharamshala town and the surrounding Dhauladhar hills. It is a result of over five years of exploration and documentation.

This book aims to be part of the evidence of the rich ecological heritage of the Kangra valley, which is at risk due to environmental changes and loss of habitat brought about by rapid, unplanned urban growth in the Himalayas.

Each of the 28 orchid species found in the region is richly illustrated with detailed photographs to describe their complex anatomy.

Whether you are keen to identify the orchids encountered on your hike or you are a serious student of botany, this guide will be a useful companion.

Meet the Author

Ashwini Bhatia writes and photographs for the Associated Press in Dharamshala, where he also runs a small cafe, Moonpeak Deli. Over the years, he has walked the surrounding hills keenly observing birds, butterflies, dragonflies, spiders and other local wildlife.

As an amateur botanist he has documented more than 500 species of flowering plants growing wild in the region. Orchids of Dharamshala is his first book.

Ashwini is currently working on a set of slim volumes on local trees of the Kangra valley.
